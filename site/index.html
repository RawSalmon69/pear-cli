Pear

Everything Pear can do.

Every tool is built on Apple’s own frameworks, so Pear stays fast and light instead of shipping its own half of macOS.

Capture

  • Screenshot⌃⇧SGrab a region into floating cards. Mark it up, copy it, or lift the background out.
  • Grab Text⌃⇧TPoint at anything on screen and the words land in your clipboard.
  • QR⌃⇧QScan a code on the screen, or turn your clipboard into a QR card.

Windows

  • Snap & ringFnSnap zones, plus a radial ring for flinging windows where you want them.
  • Dock PreviewHover a Dock icon to peek at its windows, on any display.
  • KeyClu⌃⇧KEvery shortcut for the app you’re in, gathered on one sheet.

Everyday

  • ClipboardEverything you’ve copied, searchable, with pins for the regulars.
  • Shelf⌃⇧VDrop files mid-drag, then carry them somewhere else.
  • Scratchpad⌃⇧NA note that floats above everything else.
  • Color PickerThe system eyedropper, with WCAG contrast built in.

Your Mac

  • DiskA sunburst of what’s filling your disk. Anything you remove goes to the Trash.
  • CleanupOne click of cleanup from the bundled CLI. System caches only if you turn it on.
  • MonitorCPU, memory, network, battery and sensors, at a glance.
  • RunCatA little cat in your menu bar that runs faster as the CPU heats up.
  • Menu-bar hiderTuck away icons you don’t need. Off unless you turn it on.
  • SwitchesEight system toggles in one spot.
  • Clean ModeBlank the desktop for a recording. Off unless you turn it on.

Private by design

Pear keeps everything
on your Mac.

On-device, always
Text recognition, background removal and QR scanning run on your Mac with Apple’s Vision framework. Nothing gets uploaded.
No accounts, no telemetry
Nothing to sign into, and nothing phones home. Pear does not collect anything about you.
Signed and notarized
Every build is signed with a Developer ID and notarized by Apple before it goes out.
Quiet updates
Pear updates itself over Sparkle from a signed feed, the same way plenty of trusted Mac apps do.

Pear is named after someone dear. It started as a gift, grew into a toolbox, and gets passed around the way homemade things do.
