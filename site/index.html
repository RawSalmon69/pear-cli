Pear

A drawer of small, sharp things.

Each one leans on Apple’s own frameworks instead of reinventing them — so it stays fast, quiet, and trustworthy.

Capture

  • Screenshot⌃⇧SRegion capture into floating cards — mark up, copy, or strip the background.
  • Grab Text⌃⇧TPoint at anything on screen; the words land in your clipboard.
  • QR⌃⇧QScan a code on screen, or turn your clipboard into a card.

Windows

  • Snap & ringFnSnap zones, plus a radial ring to fling windows exactly where they go.
  • Dock PreviewHover a Dock icon to peek at its windows, on any display.
  • KeyClu⌃⇧KEvery shortcut of the app you’re in, on one cheat sheet.

Everyday

  • ClipboardEverything you’ve copied, searchable, with pins.
  • Shelf⌃⇧VDrop files mid-drag, then carry them onward.
  • Scratchpad⌃⇧NA note that floats above everything else.
  • Color PickerThe system eyedropper, with WCAG contrast.

Your Mac

  • DiskA sunburst of what’s eating your space; removals go to the Trash.
  • CleanupOne-click cleanup via the bundled CLI; system caches only if you opt in.
  • MonitorCPU, memory, network, battery and sensors at a glance.
  • RunCatA menu-bar runner that trots faster as your CPU works.
  • Menu-bar hiderTuck away icons you don’t need. Off by default.
  • SwitchesEight system toggles in one place.
  • Clean ModeBlank the desktop for recordings. Off by default.

Private by architecture

No server. Nothing to
send your data to.

On-device, always
Text recognition, background removal and QR scanning run on your Mac through Apple’s Vision framework. Nothing leaves.
No accounts, no telemetry
Nothing to sign into, nothing phoning home. There’s no analytics pipeline because there’s no pipeline.
Signed & notarized
Every release is built in CI, signed with a Developer ID and notarized by Apple.
Quiet updates
Pear updates itself over Sparkle from a signed feed — the mechanism trusted Mac apps have used for years.

Named after someone dear. Started as a gift, grew into a toolbox, and is shared the way homemade things are — free, for friends, family, and now you.

No pricing page. No roadmap. Just a small thing, made carefully.

Put the drawer in your menu bar.
